Freshman Year

I don't remember much from freshman year. I know was a lot more timid then, but then again, pretty much everyone was. I had a few more friends, since then some of us have moved to different schools or drifted to other friend groups. We all still intermingle, though.
While I wasn't the most adventurous at the time, I did partake in my old middle school's drama show, which sadly got cut off due to covid. It resumed in junior year, but we'll get to that later.
Speaking of, covid for me was... interesting. I really struggled to keep myself focused on school, and my language, German, was not at all enjoyable outside of the classroom. I did, however, make tons of online friends on a minecraft server which has since been shut down, but if you want the discord, here it is: Voltage Discord. I'm actually still quite close with them today.
Covid did, however, instill a strong urge of mine to jump outside of my comfort zone. Ironically, I doubt I would have accomplished as much if I hadn't gone through so much depressing isolation in quarentine. Optimism! :D

Sophomore Year

Sophomore year was odd. I don't remember the majority of it, to be honest. I got Covid once, that was interesting. Anytime I wasn't completely passed out I was finding incredible joy in my ability to chug lemon juice like it was nothing. My stomach was not amused.
Everything extracarricular was put on hold, so I found entertainment elsewhere; Online!! Yes, a somewhat scary place, but I seem to have gotten lucky in that area. I became a moderator of the aforementioned Minecraft Server (Voltage), which was a lot of fun.
I was taught how to manipulate command blocks in game, and really enjoyed playing around with it, seeing what I could do. I believe that sparked a lot of my interest in coding. I wanted to learn how games worked, how websites worked, and how to make my own modifications to them.
And, in a way, I did! A while later I made a little doodle of a "birb", posted it to the discord for fun, and soon after recieved a DM from Voltage's programmer asking if I wanted to do the graphics for an app he was working on. I agreed, and over the summer of sophomore year we made a cute little app by the name of Joel. You can go check it out here, actually.
Back then, I didn't know as much about HTML as I do now, so I mostly kept to the graphics side of things, just guiding the dev as he wrote the code for the site. It still fascinated me seeing it all come together, though, and you'd be impressed to know that the developer of Joel was only 13 yrs old at the time.

Junior Year

Junior year can be described as nothing short of a rollercoaster. I've been stressed out of my mind, gone through a lot of heartbreak and dissapointment, and yet also had the most fun ever. I'm prideful and dissapointed all at the same time.
The summer before junior year started, I was offered a job at the Walmart in Fort Wright, which, yes, it's Walmart, but I was incredibly proud of myself. For 6 months I was recognized as one of the most hardworking employees on our team, and while some of my coworkers were less than enjoyable to be around, I genuinely loved working and the independence it gave me. I was working 26 hours a week, earning $17/hr. I worked so much that Joel, a project I once loved working on, was put on the backburner and eventually let go of entirely. During junior year I also took up AP Language. I grew to be incredibly ambitious, and my expectations of myself grew, too. That's where this begins to take a turn.
See, ambition and high expectations aren't a bad thing, in moderation. I had no such thing. In my mind, if I was not the most successful, most improved version of myself, then I was nothing at all.
I lost my job.
The week before, I had called off work for a performance I was a part of (the aforementioned drama production, which was resumed in november of this school year, rehearsals weekly), and had called off again, because of my period.
This, according to my male, very ignorant boss, was unnacceptable. I was informed of this over the phone, fired, and instructed to turn in my work phone the next day.
This, of course, was devestating. I cried a bunch, ranted about it to anyone who would listen, and basically went through the 7 stages of greif. Over walmart.
Not my brightest moment, I'll admit.

I moved on, though. Losing my job gave me time to actually pause and reflect on what I've learned this year - which is a lot. Sure, I learned some in all of my classes, but I actually remembered the most from computer science. Mostly, because I actually enjoy learning it. Outside of school, I learned how to manage my time better, enjoy my own hobbies, and take more opportunities.

I was dissapointed in myself. I deeply felt as though I failed everyone. Impostor syndrome had set in, and I felt like an absolute outsider among everyone in this school, other than in this class. Sure, I never designed a VR game like I planned to, but that's been added to the list of unrealistic goals considering how busy I've been this year.

Senior Year

In my past 3 years, I've learned a LOT. I've come a long way in maturing, and I believe I've found a subject I enjoy enough to actually follow through in (thanks to this class).
I'm definitely not done, though. My ambition has only grown stronger over the years, and I've got plans for next year that mirror that ambition.
Most notably: 2 AP and 3 dual credit courses. ':D
I'm also working on a lot of personal improvements, like focusing on my physical health more, and of course, professionally. I need a new job, lol.
I don't know how exactly I'm going to keep up with it all, if it's even possible, but I know for a fact that I'm going to put my all into it.
I believe in myself. :)
